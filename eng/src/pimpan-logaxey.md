<h1>Common Phrases and Expressions</h1>
<p>
</p>
<h2>Greetings</h2>
<p><strong>salom</strong> - hi, hello </p>
<p><strong>xanti</strong> - hello ("peace") </p>
<p><strong>bonsoba</strong> - good morning </p>
<p><strong>bonnuru</strong> - good day/afternoon </p>
<p><strong>bonaxam</strong> - good evening </p>
<p><strong>bonnoce</strong> - good evening ("good night")</p>
<h2>Farewells</h2>
<p><strong>weda</strong> - (good)bye </p>
<p><strong>xanti</strong> - bye ("peace") </p>
<p><strong>finfe (rioko)</strong> - see you </p>
<p><strong>finfe xaya</strong> - see you later </p>
<p><strong>finfe ner xaya</strong> - see you soon </p>
<p><strong>bon soba</strong> - good morning </p>
<p><strong>bon nuru</strong> - good day/afternoon </p>
<p><strong>bon axam</strong> - good evening </p>
<p><strong>bon noce</strong> - good night </p>
<h2>Other Well Wishes</h2>
<p>Note: As seen above, greetings with <strong>bon</strong> (good, well) are expressed as compounds, whereas farewells
	are expressed as two-word phrases. In the following expressions, compounds are used when the speaker and listener
	remain together. Hence, the two root words are together, combined into one word. In contrast, two-word phrases are
	used if either the speaker or the listener departs. Hence, the root words are apart. </p>
<p><strong>bonata</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>bon ata</strong> - welcome </p>
<p><strong>bonxanse</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>bon xanse</strong> - good luck </p>
<p><strong>bonyam</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>bon yam</strong> - bon appetit ("good meal") </p>
<p><strong>bonglu</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>bon glu</strong> - cheers ("good drink") </p>
<p><strong>bonturi</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>bon turi</strong> - bon voyage, safe travels </p>
<h2>Being Polite</h2>
<p><strong>fe lutuf</strong> - please </p>
<p><strong>xukra</strong> - thank you </p>
<p><strong>multi xukra</strong> - many thanks, thank you much </p>
<p><strong>no hay seba</strong> - you're welcome ("there's no reason") </p>
<p><strong>asif</strong> - sorry </p>
<p><strong>mafu</strong> - excuse me, pardon </p>

<p><strong>daybon</strong> - great, excellent </p>
<p><strong>melibon</strong> - nice, sweet </p>
<p><strong>suprem</strong> - cool, great, excellent </p>
<p><strong>otima</strong> - awesome </p>
<p><strong>afarin</strong> - well done, good job ("applause") </p>
<p><strong>hura</strong> - hooray, hoorah, hurrah, woo-hoo, yay </p>
<p><strong>ay</strong> - ouch </p>
<p><strong>wao</strong> - wow </p>

<p><strong>fe fato</strong> - in fact, actually </p>
<p><strong>fe fini</strong> - finally </p>
<p><strong>fe bonxanse</strong> - luckily, fortunately </p>
<p><strong>fe asif</strong> - regretfully, unfortunately </p>
<p><strong>fe onxala</strong> - hopefully </p>
<p><strong>fe misal</strong> - for example </p>
<p><strong>fe xugwan</strong> - usually </p>
<p><strong>fe benji</strong> - in essence, basically </p>
<p><strong>fe moy kaso</strong> - in any case, at any rate, anyway </p>
<p><strong>fe alo kaso</strong> - otherwise </p>
<p><strong>maxpul</strong> - moreover, furthermore </p>
<p><strong>pia</strong> - also </p>
<p><strong>abruto</strong> - suddenly </p>
<p><strong>e</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>em</strong> - uh </p>
<p><strong>aham</strong> - I see, understood ("understand") </p>
<p><strong>yakin</strong> - certainly, surely </p>
<p><strong>totalyakin</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>pulyakin</strong> - absolutely, totally </p>
<p><strong>mimbay</strong> - obviously, of course </p>
<p><strong>sipul</strong> - indeed </p>
<p><strong>okey</strong> - okay, all right </p>
<p><strong>ible</strong> - maybe, perhaps, possibly </p>
<p><strong>dayible</strong> - probably </p>
<p><strong>sahi</strong> - correct, right </p>
<p><strong>mal</strong> - incorrect, wrong, not so </p>
<p><strong>sati</strong> - true </p>
<p><strong>falso</strong> - false, not true </p>
<p><strong>samaijen</strong> - agreed ("same opinion") </p>
<p><strong>Kam sati?</strong> - Really? </p>
<p><strong>Kam jidi?</strong> - Seriously? Are you serious? </p>
<p><strong>Kam yakin?</strong> - Are you sure? </p>
<p><strong>Kam bon?</strong> - Are you well? Are you good? Is it good? </p>
<p><strong>Kam okey?</strong> - Is that okay? Is everything all right? </p>
<h2>Common Questions/Replies</h2>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Yu sen kepul?</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>Yu kepul?</strong><br> How are you? </li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Daymo bon, ji yu?</strong><br> Very well, and you?</p>
<p><strong>semibon</strong><br> Not too bad</p>
<p><strong>semi semi</strong><br> so-so</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Yu name keto?</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>Yusu name sen keto?</strong><br> What is your name?</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Mi name...</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>Misu name sen...</strong><br> My name is...</p>
<p><strong>(To sen) yukwe, na xorkone yu.</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>Yukwe.</strong><br> It's nice (pleasant) meeting
	you. or It's pleasant. </p>
<p><strong>(To sen) furaha, na xorkone yu.</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>Furaha.</strong><br> It's a pleasure to meet
	you. or It's a pleasure.</p>
<p><strong>(Mi sen) hox na xorkone yu.</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>Mi sen hox.</strong><br> (I am) happy to meet you.
	or I am happy.</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Yu ogar keloka?</strong><br> Where do you live?</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Mi ogar in...</strong><br> I live in...</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Yu sen of keloka?</strong><br> Where are you from?</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Mi sen of...</strong><br> I'm from...</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Yu sen kemo lao?</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>Yu sen fe ke nyan?</strong><br> How old are you? </li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Mi sen lao fe... (nyan).</strong> <em>or</em> <strong>Mi sen fe... (nyan).</strong><br> I am... years old.
</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Kam yu (pala) Globasa?</strong><br> Do you speak Globasa?</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Si, xosu.</strong><br> Yes, a (little) bit.</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Yu pala ke basa?</strong><br> What language(s) do you speak? </li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Mi pala...</strong><br> I speak...</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Kam yu aham?</strong><br> Do you understand?</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>(Si,) mi aham.</strong><br> (Yes,) I understand. </p>
<p><strong>(No,) mi no aham.</strong><br> (No,) I don't understand.</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Ren loga... kemaner (in Globasa)?</strong><br> How do you say... (in Globasa)?</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>In Globasa, ren loga...</strong><br> (In Globasa), you say... </p>

<p><strong>Mi no jixi.</strong><br> I don't know.</p>
